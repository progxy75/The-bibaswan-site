SSC CPO Examination 2023: Notification

The SSC CPO Examination is held in order to hire candidates for the positions of Sub Inspector in Delhi Police, Sub Inspector in CAPFs, and Assistant Sub Inspector in CISF, Inspector jobs, and Sub Inspector jobs. The Staff Selection Commission will release the SSC CPO Examination 2023 Official Notification on their website on July 20, 2023. The SSC CPO 2023 online application form fill up will be closed on August 13, 2023. The Sub-Inspector in Delhi Police and Central Armed Police Forces Examination, 2023, will be held from October 3rd to October 6th, 2023. All more information regarding the SSC CPO Recruitment 2023 will be posted on our website after the SSC CPO 2023 Official Notification is published by the Commission.

SSC CPO Examination 2023: Apply Online

The SSC CPO 2023 online registration will start after the release of the SSC CPO 2023 Notification. Candidates with the required educational qualifications may apply for the position of Sub Inspector in the Central Armed Police Forces.

SSC CPO Examination 2023: Application Fees

  • Candidates must pay a fee of Rs. 100/- (except for female/SC/ST candidates who are excused from paying a fee).
  • Fees can be paid using an SBI Challan, SBI Net Banking, or any bank’s credit or debit card.
  • SBI challans may be paid at any branch of the bank during normal business hours by candidates who wish to use them.

SSC CPO Examination 2023: Eligibility Criteria

a) Educational Qualification

  • A Bachelor’s Degree in any stream from a recognised university is required.
  • Male candidates must have a valid Driving Licence for LMV (Motorcycle and Car) for the position of Sub Inspector in Delhi Police (alone) as of the date set for Physical Endurance and Standard Tests. Otherwise, they will be rejected to appear for the Physical Endurance and Standard Tests.

 

b) Age Criteria
c) Minimum Age: 20 years
d) Maximum Age: 25 years

The candidates should not be born before 02.01.1997 and not after 01.01.2002

SSC CPO Recruitment 2023: Medical Examination

The Medical Officer of the CAPFs, as well as any other Medical Officer or Assistant Surgeon belonging to Grade I of any Central/State Government Hospital or Dispensary, would conduct a medical examination on all candidates who will qualify the SSC CPO PET. Unsuitable candidates will be notified of their status and given a 15-day window to appeal. The decision of the Re-Medical Board/Review Medical Board will not be appealed; it will be considered final.

There would be 200 Multiple Choice Questions in SSC CPO Paper 1. During the examination each correct answer is worth one mark, while each incorrect answer will deduct one-third of a mark. The SSC CPO 2023 Paper 1 will last 2 hours (120 minutes).

Take a fast glance at all of the questions within 2 minutes. You would then know the overall level of the section. Now you can answer the questions you know the answers to. Answer each question in 30 seconds or less.

Paper I of the SSC CPO Syllabus is broken into four sections: General Intelligence and Reasoning, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude, and English Comprehension.
Questions in this section will assess the candidate's grasp and knowledge of the English language and will be based on:

• Error recognition,
• Filling in the blanks (using verbs, prepositions articles, etc),
• Vocabulary,
• Spellings,
• Grammar,
• Sentence Structure,
• Synonyms,
• Antonyms,
• Sentence Completion,
• Phrases and Idiomatic Use of Words,
• Comprehension etc.

Each subject will have 50 questions, each worth one mark. There is a negative marking scheme in the exam, with 0.25 points deducted for each incorrect answer.
