West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service

The WBPSC (West Bengal Public Service Commission) has published the eligibility criteria for the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Examination on its official website. Each applicant aspiring to apply for the position must meet the Eligibility. The Eligibility Criteria in the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service encompass factors such as age limit, educational qualification, citizenship, experience, and more.

  • Candidates will be rejected who will fail to meet the eligibility criteria for the Audit and Accounts service post
  • Carefully review the entire article to verify the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Eligibility and determine if you meet all the requirements before submitting your application

Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility Criteria

 

West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Eligibility

Age

Candidates who are applying for the WBPSC recruitment must not be more than 36 years

Educational Qualification

Candidates must hold a Bachelor’s Degree in Commerce from a recognized university or must be a Member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India or must be Member of the Institute of Cost Accountants of India or MBA/PGDM (FINANCE) or its equivalent post-graduation degree in Finance under 2 years full-time regular course approved by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE).

Nationality 

A person having citizenship of India or such a person of other nationality as declared eligible by the Government of India.

Number of Attempts

Until crossing the maximum eligible age

Experience

Freshets’ are also eligible. 

Age Limit

  • The age criteria of the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Examination states that candidates must be below 36 years old as of 01.01.2022 (i.e., born on or after 2nd January 1986).
  • Individuals who possess the necessary qualifications and are below the age of 36 years can submit their applications.

Educational Qualification

Below, you will find the prescribed educational qualifications that candidates must meet in order to be eligible for the position of Audit and Account Services.

  • Candidates must possess a Bachelor’s Degree from a recognised university.
  • The applicant should be an affiliate of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India.
  • The applicant should be an affiliate of the Institute of Cost Accountants of India
  • The applicant should hold an MBA/PGDM (FINANCE) or a corresponding postgraduate degree in Finance or have completed a two-year full-time regular course sanctioned by the All-India Council for Technical Education.

Nationality

 Candidates from all over the country have the eligibility to apply for the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service post,provided they fulfil the remaining mandatory requirements. The West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service position is open to the subsequent applicants w ho is a citizen of India or an individual of another nationality as approved by the Government of India.

Number of Attempts

Candidates are granted the freedom to make unlimited attempts in pursuit of the job as long as candidates adhere to the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Examination age limit, which is 36 years for the general category and considering the concessions offered for other categories.

Experience

Candidates who fulfill the eligibility criteria’s given by the commission, can apply for the position of West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Officer, even without any prior experience.

Physical Measurement

The official notification does not specify any explicit provisions. However, a medical assessment will be conducted, and candidates chosen for appointments will need to appear before a Medical Board to obtain certificates confirming their suitability for Government service.

Important Points

When applying for the position of West Bengal Audit and Account Service officer, the candidates should bear in mind the following considerations.

  • Fluency in Bengali language, including reading, writing, and speaking, is mandatory for all candidates, except those whose mother tongue is Nepali.
  • Irrespective of their category, candidates from other States will be considered as candidates belonging to the General category.
  • Only the date of birth recorded in the Madhyamika or equivalent certificate will be accepted. The age limits will apply to all candidates, whether currently in Government Service or not.
  • Candidates with locomotor disabilities or Cerebral Palsy who are unable to write the exam are eligible to avail the assistance of a scribe. However, the candidate must make arrangements for the scribe. Such candidates will be granted an additional compensatory time of 20 minutes.
  • Applications must be submitted online. Multiple applications from the same candidate are strictly prohibited and will result in rejection.
  • The preliminary examination is scheduled to take place on 4th June 2023. The commission will soon issue the admit card for the aforementioned examination.

Note: A total of 25 vacancies have been made available for recruitment. Aspiring candidates can refer to the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Previous Year Papers, which provide valuable insights and aid in achieving success in the examination.

Points to be Considered for Mains Examination

Below, you will find the comprehensive outline of the main examination format for the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service.

  • The Mains Exam will encompass a total of 800 marks.
  • The format of the paper will be Descriptive.
  • The examination will consist of mandatory papers as well as elective papers.
  • Both the Compulsory and Optional papers will be carrying 100 marks each and will have a duration of 3 hours.
  • The Optional Papers’ difficulty level will be equivalent to that of an Honours Degree Examination as stipulated by a recognized Indian University/Institute.
  • Candidates should have the flexibility to write their answers in either English or Bengali language for all the papers.
  • For answer papers in Hindi or Nepali, candidates are permitted to use the Devanagari script. Questions for the Santali Paper will be provided in the Olchiki script, and candidates must write their answers in the same script.
  • The Final Merit List will be formulated based on the cumulative marks obtained in the West Bengal Audit and Accounts Service Main Examination and the personality test.

Probation Period

The probation period in the WBPSC denotes the duration in which candidates must serve in the Commission to become eligible for permanent employment. Throughout this period, candidates  performance and work ethic are assessed by senior officials. The typical probation period in the government sector spans 2 years, with the initial 6 months dedicated to training. After this period, candidates can avail themselves of all the privileges and advantages associated with the job.

Job Profile

The primary responsibilities of a WB Audit & Accounts Service Officer involves the execution of governmental financial policies. These officers are entrusted with the crucial task of diligently overseeing the allocation and utilization of funds from the Government exchequer, ensuring their optimal utilization in the best interest of the public.
