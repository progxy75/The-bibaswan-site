What is the SBI PO Full Form?

SBI PO stands for State Bank of India Probationary Officer. SBI conducts a competitive exam known as the SBI PO Exam to select eligible candidates’ aptitude, reasoning ability, general awareness and English language proficiency. those who pass the exam go through subsequent rounds of group discussion and personal interview to finalize the selection. Once selected as an SBI PO, individuals are appointed as Probationary Officers in different branches of the bank.

SBI PO Eligibility Criteria 2023

To apply for the SBI PO exam, it is essential to meet the SBI PO Eligibility Criteria. Before applying for the exam, make sure to check the eligibility criteria for SBI PO 2023. Fulfilling the following conditions is necessary to avoid application cancellation later on. Take a look at the detailed eligibility criteria, including age limit, educational qualifications, and more. Ensure that you meet all the requirements before proceeding with the application process.

SBI PO Nationality

To be eligible for the SBI PO exam, candidates must fulfill certain citizenship criteria. The eligibility requirements include being a citizen of India, or a subject of Nepal or Bhutan. Additionally, candidates must adhere to the government rules and regulations of India. These criteria ensure that only eligible candidates can apply for the SBI PO exam.

SBI PO Age Limit

The State Bank of India has set specific age limit criteria for SBI PO recruitment. Candidates must fulfill the following age requirements: • Minimum age limit: 21 years • Maximum age limit: 30 years These age limits are determined by SBI officials and serve as a guideline for the eligibility of candidates applying for the SBI PO exam.

SBI PO Age Relaxation

The SBI PO recruitment process includes certain relaxations and reservations for specific categories. Here are some key points to note:

  • Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD) are eligible for a 4% horizontal reservation.
  • There is a 5-year age relaxation for candidates belonging to SC/ST/EXSM categories.
  • For OBC candidates, there is a 3-year age relaxation, while for PwBD candidates, the age relaxation is 10 years.

These relaxations and reservations aim to provide equal opportunities and inclusivity in the SBI PO recruitment process. Candidates belonging to these categories can benefit from these provisions while fulfilling the eligibility criteria.

SBI PO Educational Qualification

To be eligible for SBI PO, candidates must meet certain educational qualifications. Here are the key points regarding the educational criteria:

  • Graduation in any discipline from a recognized University is required.
  • An equivalent qualification recognized by the Central Government is also accepted.
  • It is essential to have a fair knowledge of computers.
  • The date mentioned on the mark-sheet or provisional certificate issued by the University/Institute will be considered as the date of passing the eligibility examination.

These criteria ensure that candidates possess the necessary educational background and computer knowledge required for the SBI PO position.

SBI PO Selection Process

The selection process for SBI PO consists of three stages:

  1. Preliminary Exam (Objective): This is the first stage of the selection process and serves as a qualifying round.
  2. Mains Exam (Objective + Descriptive): The second stage includes a comprehensive mains examination consisting of both objective and descriptive questions.
  3. Group Exercise and Interview: The final stage involves a group exercise and interview, where candidates are assessed based on their communication skills, leadership qualities, and overall personality.

The merit list for the SBI PO selection is prepared based on the marks obtained in the mains examination and the performance in the group exercise and interview. The preliminary exam acts as a qualifying round and its marks are not considered for the final merit list.

Biometric Verification

To ensure the security of the SBI PO 2023 selection process, a Biometric verification system is being introduced. This involves the bank taking your digital thumb impression to verify your authenticity. It is important to note that any inconsistency in your biometric data can result in rejection and may even lead to legal consequences. Therefore, it is recommended not to apply substances like Mehndi (Henna), Chemicals, Ink, etc., on your hands to avoid any issues during the biometric verification process.

  2. Selected candidates can download the interview call letter from the official website.
  3. The interview session takes place after the Mains examination.
  4. The Group Discussion carries a weightage of 20 marks, while the Personal Interview carries a weightage of 30 marks.

It is essential for candidates to prepare well for the interview round as it plays a crucial role in the final selection process for SBI PO.

SBI PO Final Selection

Here are some key points to understand the scoring and selection process for SBI PO:

  1. The Preliminary round scores do not contribute to the final scores. However, clearing this stage is essential to progress further in the selection process.
  2. The Mains round scores are crucial for determining the final result. These scores carry significant weightage.
  3. The final scores are calculated by combining the Mains round scores and the interview round scores. The merit lists are prepared based on these cumulative scores.
  4. Due to the impact of Covid, Phase 3 may consist of either an interview (50 marks) or a combination of interview (30 marks) and group discussion (20 marks).
  5. Candidates who secure a place in the final merit lists will be offered the position of Probationary Officers.

It is important for candidates to perform well in both the Mains round and the interview round to increase their chances of being recruited as Probationary Officers.
