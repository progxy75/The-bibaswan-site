IBPS RRB 2023 Eligibility Criteria

To apply for IBPS RRB 2023, candidates need to fulfil certain eligibility criteria. These criteria consist of educational qualifications, age limits, and any required experience. Let’s take a detailed look at the eligibility criteria:

  1. Educational Qualification: Candidates must fulfil the required educational qualifications as specified by IBPS for each post. The specific educational requirements vary based on the position applied for, such as Office Assistant, Officer Scale I, II, or III.
  2. Age Limit: There are age limits set by IBPS for each category of candidates. The minimum and maximum age limits differ depending on the post applied for. It is very important to check the official notification for the specific age criteria applicable to each position.
  3. Experience: In some cases, candidates may be required to have relevant work experience in the banking sector. The nature and duration of the experience required vary based on the post and the specific requirements mentioned in the notification.

Candidates should carefully review and ensure that they meet all the eligibility criteria specified by IBPS RRB 2023 before proceeding with the application process.

The age limit for IBPS RRB (as of 01/06/2023) varies according to the different categories:

  • For Officer Scale-III: Candidates should be between 21 and 40 years of age. • For Officer Scale-II: Candidates should be between 21 and 32 years of age. • For Officer Scale-I: Candidates should be between 18 and 30 years of age. • For Office Assistant (Multipurpose): Candidates should be between 18 and 30 years of age.

IBPS also provides certain age relaxations for specific categories. The information of age relaxation are as follows:

  • Scheduled Caste/ Scheduled Tribe: 05 years of age relaxation. • Other Backward Class: 03 years of age relaxation. • Person with disability: 10 years of age relaxation. • Ex-Serviceman/Disabled Ex-Serviceman: the actual time spent serving in the armed services plus three more years (eight additional years for disabled ex-servicemen who are SC/ST members), up to a maximum age of fifty. • A 9-year age relaxation applies to widows, divorcees, and women who have been legally separated from their spouses and are single.

Candidates falling under any of these categories may be eligible for the specified age relaxations as per the IBPS RRB 2023 guidelines.

IBPS RRB Selection Process

The IBPS RRB 2023 Examination is divided into two stages: Prelims and Main. On the other hand, the IBPS PO or Officer Scale I examination consists of three stages: Prelims, Mains, and Interviews. For IBPS Officer Grade II (General Banking Officer and Specialist Cadre) and III, there is a single-window exam called the IBPS Officer Grade II Exam, followed by an Interview. To secure a position in any of these posts, candidates need to successfully pass each stage of the respective examinations.

IBPS RRB Exam Pattern

The IBPS RRB Exam Pattern varies depending on the post you are applying for, namely Office Assistant/Clerk and Officer Scale. Therefore, the examination process differs for each of these positions.

IBPS RRB Office Assistant Exam Pattern

For the IBPS RRB Office Assistant Exam, there are two phases:
1. Preliminary Exam
2. Mains Exam
Candidates applying for the Office Assistant position will not undergo an interview process. Selection will be based solely on the candidate’s performance in the Mains Examination.
In the exam, 0.25 marks will be reduced for every response marked by the candidate. The test will be objective in nature.

IBPS RRB Syllabus 2023

The syllabus for the IBPS RRB 2023 exam is mostly similar for both the IBPS RRB Assistant and IBPS RRB Officer positions. However, the Specialist Officer Grade-II candidates will have an additional section called Professional Knowledge. The topics covered in the IBPS RRB Exam syllabus are as follows:

1. Quantitative Aptitude
2. Logical Reasoning
3. English/Hindi Language
4. Financial Knowledge
5. General Awareness
6. Computer Knowledge

IBPS RRB Logical Reasoning Syllabus includes topics such as Odd man out, Analogy, Syllogism, Coding-Decoding, Blood Relation, Alphabet Test, Series Test, Ranking and Time, Causes and Effects, Direction Test, Sitting Arrangements, Decision Making, Statement and Assumption, Figure Series, Assertion and Reason, Statement and Conclusion, Word Formation, Statements and Action Courses, Inequalities, and Puzzles.

IBPS RRB Quantitative Aptitude Syllabus covers Number System, HCF and LCM, Profit and Loss, Decimal Fractions, Simple Interest, Compound Interest, Time and Work, Time and Distance, Average, Age Problems, Simplification, Partnership, Percentage, Ratio and Proportion, Data Interpretation, Permutation and Combination, Probability, and Quadratic Equations.

For the English/Hindi Language section, the syllabus includes topics like Rearrangement of Sentences, Fill in the Blanks, Error Detection, Cloze Test, Jumbled Words, and Idioms, One-word substitution, Phrase Substitution, Antonyms, and Synonyms. Candidates opting for Hindi can also expect topics such as Vyakaran, Error Spotting, Paragraphs, and Reading Comprehension.

In the Computer Knowledge section, candidates should be familiar with topics such as Computer basics, abbreviations, terminology for hardware and software, shortcuts, networking, knowledge of the internet at a basic level, MS Office, and databases, History of Computers, Security Tools, Number System and Conversions, Computer Languages, Internet, and Input and Output Devices.

The General Awareness syllabus includes India and International Current Affairs, Banking Awareness, Countries and Currencies, National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries, Banking Terms and Abbreviations, Banking History, RBI, Sports, Finance, Books and Authors, Agriculture, Fiscal Policies, Government Schemes, and Government Policies.

The financial Awareness syllabus covers the Latest Topics in News in the Financial World, Monetary Policy, Budget, and Economic Survey, Overview of Banking and Banking Reforms in India, Bank Accounts and Special Individuals, Organizations Deposits Credit, Loans, Advanced Non-Performing Assets, Asset Reconstruction Companies, NPAs, Restructuring of Loans, Bad Loans, Risk Management, BASEL I, BASEL II, and BASEL III.

Candidates should prepare these topics thoroughly to perform well in the IBPS RRB Exam.
