IB ACIO

The IB ACIO (Intelligence Bureau Assistant Central Intelligence Officer) is an examination conducted at the graduation level, under the auspices of the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) in India. This prestigious examination opens the gateway to a high-salaried position within the Government of India, accompanied by various allowances.

Initially, the Intelligence Bureau (IB) served as the focal point for both internal and external intelligence functions within the country. However, over time, it underwent a division, giving rise to two distinct entities: the Intelligence Bureau and the Research & Analysis Wing (R&AW).

IB ACIO Recruitment 2023: Exam Pattern

 
The IB ACIO recruitment exam is conducted in two stages: Paper I and Paper II. In order to excel in the examination, candidates should possess a comprehensive understanding of the syllabus and relevant topics. The exam pattern for IB ACIO 2023 is as follows:

IB ACIO Stage I Exam Pattern 2023: The first stage is a written test, carrying a total of 100 marks.

  • It comprises multiple-choice questions (MCQs) and encompasses four sections: General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude, Logical Ability, and English Language.
  • Candidates are given 60 minutes to complete the first stage.

IB ACIO Stage II Exam Pattern 2023: The second stage includes an essay writing and comprehension test, designed to be a descriptive paper.

  • This stage is divided into two sections: essay writing and comprehension, as well as précis writing.
  • The essay section carries 30 marks, while comprehension and précis writing contribute 20 marks, making a total of 50 marks.
  • Candidates are allocated 60 minutes for Stage 2.

Following successful completion of both stages, candidates proceed to the interview phase, where their personality, intelligence, awareness, and knowledge are evaluated. The interview holds a total of 100 marks.

IB ACIO Recruitment Examination 2023: Age Limit

The age criteria for appearing in the IB ACIO 2023 exam are as follows:

  • The minimum age requirement is 18 years.
  • The maximum age limit is 27 years.

Age relaxation is provided for certain categories:

  • SC candidates have a relaxation of 5 years in the upper age limit.
  • ST candidates have a relaxation of 3 years in the upper age limit.
  • Departmental candidates with at least 3 years of service have an upper age limit of 40 years.
  • Widows/Divorced/Separated women who are not remarried have an upper age limit of 35 years (for General category).
  • Widows/Divorced/Separated women who are not remarried have an upper age limit of 40 years (for SC/ST/OBC categories).

IB ACIO Recruitment Examination 2023: Application Form

Candidates must carefully review the eligibility criteria before applying for the examination. The application process for IB ACIO involves online submission of the application form. For candidates who find it challenging to fill out the form, the following steps are provided as guidance:

IB ACIO Registration Process:

  1. Go to the official website https://www.mha.gov.in/.
  2. Look for the new registration notification and click on it.
  3. Fill in the registration form with details like name, father’s name, mother’s name, etc.
  4. Click “submit” and note down the system-generated registration ID and password.

IB ACIO Application Form:

  1. Log in to the official website using your registration ID and password.
  2. Provide all required details, including educational information, examination center, etc.
  3. Upload scanned copies of your passport-sized photograph and signature.
  4. Review the application form and make necessary corrections if needed.

IB ACIO Application Fees:

  1. To submit the application form, candidates need to pay the application fees through online mode.
  2. The application fee for General/OBC (Male candidates) is Rs 100/-, along with recruitment processing charges of Rs 500/-.
  3. Female candidates, SC, and ST candidates are exempted from paying the fees.
  4. Online payment methods such as credit/debit cards, net banking, UPI, etc., can be used.
  5. Candidates should keep a print-out of the payment receipt and application form for future reference.
