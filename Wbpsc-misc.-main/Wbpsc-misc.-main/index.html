WBPSC Miscellaneous 2023: Application Form

The process for applying for the WBPSC Miscellaneous is online. The application link will be activated after the release of the official notification. You can apply by following these steps:

  1. Visit the WBPSC official website and click on the link to apply for the WBPSC Miscellaneous Exam.
  2. Register yourself (if you haven’t already) and log in.
  3. Fill in all the required details. Upload your images, signature, and make sure to mention your category.
  4. Pay the application fee and then submit the application form.

WBPSC Miscellaneous 2023: Syllabus

Candidates who will sit for the WBPSC Miscellaneous Examination 2023 need to have a proper understanding of the syllabus in order to succeed. WBPSC Miscellaneous 2023: Prelims Syllabus

a. General Knowledge: The goal is to assess the candidates knowledge in several spheres:

  • Well-known places and people in the news
  • Indian Politics and the Constitution
  • The Indian Economy
  • Current Events (Both National and International)
  • Indian History including the Struggle for Freedom
  • Geography: Rivers, Relief Features, Geographic Facts, and so on.
  • Important National and International Events
  • Application of Science in Daily Life
  • Art and Culture related information from West Bengal
  • Traditional General Knowledge

b. Arithmetic: This section will be based on the WBSE M.E. exam syllabus.

WBPSC Miscellaneous 2023: Mains Syllabus

Paper 1: English:

  • Report Drafting
  • Translating from (Bengali/Hindi/Urdu/Nepali/Santhali) to English.
  • Summary writing from a prose passage.
  • Fill in the blanks and proper usage of vocabulary. Correction of sentences, Synonyms, antonyms,

Paper 2: Bengali/Hindi/Urdu/Santali/Nepali

  • Report drafting from materials supplied.
  • Translating from English to Bengali/Hindi/Urdu/Nepali/Santali.
  • Summary writing from a prose passage.

Paper 3: General Studies & Arithmetic of class 10th (M.E. Level)

WBPSC Miscellaneous 2023: Selection Process

WBPSC Miscellaneous 2023 selection procedure includes three stages: prelims, mains, and interview. Candidates qualifying the Prelims Examination can appear for the Main Examination. After that the merit list of the Main Examination will be considered for the selection of candidates for the Personal Interview Round.

A candidate who will qualify all the three stages, will be finally selected for the job.

WBPSC Miscellaneous 2023: Interview

The final stage is the personal interview. It carries 100 Marks. Candidates who will qualify the WBPSC Miscellaneous Mains Exam will be allowed to appear for the WBPSC Miscellaneous Personal Interview round. The marks from both the main and personal interview will be combined to generate the final merit list, from which candidates will be selected for the job.

FAQ’S

Public Service Commission, West Bengal conducts the Miscellaneous Services Recruitment Examination to recruit candidates for various posts. It takes place annually or when the Commission announces the date. The Miscellaneous exam is a 3-stage examination - Preliminary, Final, and Personality Test.

The candidate must have a graduate degree from any recognised University or its equivalent as per the Miscellaneous 2019 notification educational qualification criteria. Additionally, the aspirant should have the ability to read, write and speak in Bengali (not required for those whose mother tongue is Nepali).

The WBPSC Miscellaneous salary is same for all the posts covered under this examination. WBPSC Miscellaneous 2019 pay scale was Rs. 7,100 - 37,600/- + Grade Pay of Rs. 3,900/- (or 3,600/-0).

A modification will be made to the WBPSC Miscellaneous Services Salary structure in 2023. The updated salary structure will be included in the official notification.

According to the last notification released by the Commission for Miscellaneous 2019 Exam, the age of the candidates should be not below 20 years but not more than 39 years on 01.01.2019.

Concession in age
S.C. & S.T. candidates can get an age relaxation of 5 years and B.C. (Non-creamy layer) candidates are eligible for 3 year age relaxation. In the case of Persons with Disabilities (PWD), the upper age limit is 45 years. S.C./S.T./B.C. candidates of other States shall be treated as general candidates and no relaxation is applicable for them.
Date of birth as recorded only in theMadhyamikor equivalent certificate, will be accepted.
The age limits apply to all the candidates whether in Government service or not.
